Shrek's life

Once upon a time, there was a little ogre, who lived with his parents in a bog by a tree. It was a pretty nast place, but he was happy because ogres like nasty. On his 7th birthday, the little ogre’s parents sat him down to talk, just as all ogre parents had for hundreds of years before. “The rules are very clear. Now you’re seven, so it's time to go away.” And so the little ogre went along his way. And so the little ogre found a perfectly rancid swamp far away from civilization. And whenever a mob came along to burn him at the stake, he knew exactly what to do. Shrek the ogre was happy once again.

One day Lord Farquaad hoofed und poofed, und he signed an evection notice for all the fairy tale creatures. They were dumped on swamp with nowhere to go and if they returned to Lord Farquaad’s kingdom they would be murdered. The fairytale creatures convince Shrek to speak to Lord Farquaad in hopes they can return to the kingdom.
On Shrek’s journey to Lord Farquaad’s kingdom, Duloc, he encounters (an annoying) talking Donkey. The Donkey befriends Shrek and leads him to the kingdom of Duloc. Here, Shrek is tangled into taking another adventure; this time it's for an exchangement. Shrek is to rescue and return the Princess of Fiona to Lord Farquaad and in exchange Lord Farquaad will give Shrek the deed to the swamp.
Shrek and Donkey head on their adventure to rescue the princess. During their adventure they face a lava river, a dragon, and a high tower to climb. Eventually, Shrek retrieves Princess Fiona, not a knightly way, and they set out to the kingdom of Duloc. During their return Shrek and Fiona’s attraction towards each other quickly grows, however through a misunderstanding it does not further.
They finally reach the kingdom; Fiona agrees to marry Farquaad and Shrek gets his deed to the swamp. The End!.... Actually, the story does not end there. In fate, Shrek regrets allowing Fiona to go off to marry Lord Farquaad without telling her how he truly feels. Shrek (and Donkey) quickly rush to the kingdom of Duloc where Shrek barges into the Church, mid-wedding, and confesses his love for Fiona. She runs off with Shrek and Donkey, leaving tiny Farquaad at the altar.
Shrek and Fiona marry each other and take a romantic honeymoon. After returning from their honeymoon and showing home movies to their friends (donkey and dragon). They quickly learn that Fiona’s parents have heard that she has married her true love. Fiona’s parents invite the couple over to their kingdom, called Far Far Away.
Fiona’s parents do not know that the curse still lingers on Fiona and that she had married an ogre, they assumed that she was wedded to prince charming and human once again. At the end of their visit in the kingdom of Far Far Away Shrek and his inlaws are finally friends and they even “Livin’ La Vida Loca”
When King Harold suddenly croaks, Shreklearns he will have to rule the land of Far, Far Away, unless he can find a suitable heir to the throne. The most-promising candidate is Princess Fiona's cousin Artie , a teenage slacker in a medieval high-school. Shrek and his trusted companions, Donkey (Eddie Murphy) and Puss in Boots), set out to bring Artie back but find their mission is a bigger challenge than they expected.
Long-settled into married life and fully domesticated, Shrek begins to long for the days when he felt like a real ogre. Duped into signing a contract with devious Rumpelstiltskin, he finds himself in an alternate version of Far Far Away, where ogres are hunted, Rumpelstiltskin rules, and he and Fiona have never met. Shrek must find a way out of the contract to restore his world and reclaim his true love.

Family Tree
Shrek was born from a wedded ogre couple; Pappa and Mama Orge. Shrek Married Princess Fiona who was born from King Harold and Queen Lillian. Shrek and Fiona had triples: Fregus, Farkle, and Felicia. Shrek’s best friend is Donkey and Donkey fell in love with Dragon. Dragon and Donkey had six hybrid donkey-dragons named Debbie, Coco, Bananas, Peanut, Parfait and Eclair.
Shrek's First Movie
Once upon a time, in a far away swamp, there lived an ogre named Shrek whose precious solitude is suddenly shattered by an invasion of annoying fairy tale characters. They were all banished from their kingdom by the evil Lord Farquaad. Determined to save their home -- not to mention his -- Shrek cuts a deal with Farquaad and sets out to rescue Princess Fiona to be Farquaad's bride. Rescuing the Princess may be small compared to her deep, dark secret.
Shrek's Second Movie
After returning from their honeymoon and showing home movies to their friends, Shrek and Fiona learn that her parents have heard that she has married her true love and wish to invite him to their kingdom, called Far Far Away. The catch is: Fiona's parents are unaware of the curse that struck their daughter and have assumed she married Prince Charming, not a 700-pound ogre with horrible hygiene and a talking donkey pal.
Shrek's Third Movie
When King Harold suddenly croaks, Shrek learns he will have to rule the land of Far, Far Away, unless he can find a suitable heir to the throne. The most-promising candidate is Princess Fiona's cousin Artie, a teenage slacker in a medieval high-school. Shrek and his trusted companions, Donkey and Puss in Boots, set out to bring Artie back but find their mission is a bigger challenge than they expected.

Shrek the Musical
Set in a mythical “once upon a time” sort of land, Shrek the Musical is the story of a hulking green ogre who, after being mocked and feared his entire life by anything that crosses his path, retreats to an ugly green swamp to exist in happy isolation. Suddenly, a gang of homeless fairy-tale characters (Pinocchio, Cinderella, the Three Pigs, you name it) raid his sanctuary, saying they’ve been evicted by the vertically challenged Lord Farquaad. So Shrek strikes a deal: I’ll get your homes back, if you give me my home back! But when Shrek and Farquaad meet, the Lord strikes a deal of his own: He’ll give the fairy-tale characters their homes back, if Shrek rescues Princess Fiona. Shrek obliges, yet finds something appealing–something strange and different–about this pretty princess. He likes her. A lot. But why does she always run off when the sun sets?
ShrekFest

Simply put: a FREAKING EPIC party my friends and I throw every year in Madison, WI. Truly the ultimate experience for Shrek-heads of all ages. If the "Shrek" part is confusing to you (very common, don't worry) just focus on the "fest" part: live music, free beer, food trucks- not to mention the games and prizes, highlighted by our infamous onion eating contest. Still confused? Come check it out for yourself. Blast your friends a group text, put together a costume (of anything really... someone dressed as Austin Powers last year) and bring a chair. We'll see ya in the swamp ;)

To everyone that's believed in the fest, from back in 2014 to those discovering it just now, with all the love we can possibly convey, thank you. We truly believe we've created something special with this festival, and are only able to keep pursuing this wacky dream with your love and support. For a few hours a year, we can create a place where it's okay to be weird, to be different, to be yourself, and to hang out with others who feel the same way. Whether it's talking to a friend you came with from a short distance away, meeting someone who came from across the globe, or anything and everything in between, it is with indescribable joy that we can bring people from all ages and all walks of life together for one fun filled day.
